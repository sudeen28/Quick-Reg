What is the minimum share capital for a foreign-owned company in Nigeria?
+
Foreign-owned companies in Nigeria require a minimum paid-up share capital of ₦100 Million Naira (approx. $65,000 USD) as mandated by the NIPC under Section 18 of the NIPC Act. QuickReg guides you through the entire structuring process.
Do I need to travel to Nigeria to incorporate my company?
+
No. QuickReg's process is 100% remote. All KYC, document submission, and regulatory filings are handled digitally. Your Certificate of Incorporation and all documents are delivered electronically.
What is NIPC registration and why is it mandatory?
+
The Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission (NIPC) registration is mandatory for all foreign-owned businesses in Nigeria. It grants your company the right to operate, repatriate profits, and access foreign exchange through the CBN.
How long does the full incorporation process take?
+
Standard CAC incorporation takes 48–72 hours. With NIPC registration, the full process typically takes 5–10 business days. We keep you updated at every stage.
Can I repatriate my profits and capital from Nigeria?
+
Yes. Foreign investors registered with the NIPC are entitled to freely repatriate capital, dividends, and profits through CBN-licensed banks. QuickReg ensures your documents are structured to support this from day one.
What currencies and payment methods do you accept?
+
We accept GBP, USD, EUR, CAD, NGN, AED and more via bank wire transfer, Wise, or Paystack. Contact us for your preferred payment method before proceeding.
